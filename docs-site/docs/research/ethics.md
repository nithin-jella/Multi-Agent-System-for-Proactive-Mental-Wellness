# Ethics and Clinical Governance

Deploying an autonomous agent in the domain of mental health requires stringent ethical frameworks and absolute adherence to clinical safety boundaries. UGM-AICare is engineered under the principle that AI is a tool for augmentation and triage, not a replacement for licensed human professionals in crisis scenarios.

## 1. Human-in-the-Loop Escalation

The system's foremost directive is to do no harm. When the Safety Triage Agent (STA) detects a Level 3 Risk (indicative of active crisis, severe self-harm, or suicidal ideation), the system enforces a strict, un-bypassable **Human-in-the-Loop (HITL)** protocol.

- The Case Management Agent (CMA) instantly takes control of the conversation.
- Autonomous therapeutic generation is suspended.
- A clinical case is immediately generated and flagged for on-call human counselors via the administrator dashboard.
- The system provides the user with emergency contact resources until a human counselor intervenes.

## 2. Evidence-Based Interventions

All automated responses generated by the Therapeutic Coach Agent (TCA) for Level 1 and Level 2 risks are strictly constrained to established Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) principles and psychoeducation. The system is programmed to explicitly refuse providing medical diagnoses or prescribing medication.

## 3. Privacy, Anonymity, and Data Sovereignty

Mental health disclosures are highly sensitive. UGM-AICare implements rigorous technical safeguards to ensure data sovereignty and user privacy:

- **PII Redaction Pipeline:** Before any conversation log is stored in the database or forwarded to the Insights Agent, the STA runs a scrub process to remove Personally Identifiable Information (names, phone numbers, specific addresses).
- **k-Anonymity (k $\ge$ 5):** The Insights Agent provides population-level analytics to university administrators. The system programmatically blocks any query that would return a dataset of fewer than 5 individuals, preventing deanonymization through hyper-specific filtering (e.g., "stress levels of 6th-semester students in the Information Engineering department").
- **Differential Privacy:** To further protect against reverse engineering, noise injection is applied to sensitive aggregate queries.

## 4. Consent and Auditability

Transparency is critical to building trust.
- Users must explicitly consent to the covert screening mechanism during onboarding.
- The system maintains an immutable audit trail of user consents, data deletions, and high-risk case escalations.
- The AIKA Autopilot governance matrix ensures all high-impact operational decisions require explicit human authorization before execution.
